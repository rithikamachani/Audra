The story
👴
It started with someone who loves music
Music had always been a constant — until a hearing aid changed everything. The frequencies that carry the warmth of a melody, the shimmer of a cymbal, the brightness of a voice — all quietly filtered out. The music was still playing. But something essential was missing.
🔍
The gap nobody had fixed
Most music apps are built for people who can hear everything. Hearing aids filter out specific frequency ranges — and nobody had designed around that gap. Not streaming platforms. Not accessibility tools. Not even AI-powered solutions that claimed to help.
🎹
Music as a foundation for accessible design
Music taught us something: communication doesn't require a single channel. A melody can be felt, not just heard. Rhythm can be seen, not just counted. Emotion can be read in a color, not just a chord. Audra was built on that idea — that every dimension of music can be translated into something accessible to every kind of listener.
🛠
Built through iteration, shaped by real feedback
Audra wasn't designed once and shipped. It went through multiple JavaScript iterations, each one tested by a real hearing-impaired listener. What confused them became the next fix. What moved them became the next feature. That loop — build, test, listen, rebuild — is still how Audra grows.
What Audra does
Frequency landscape
Real-time waveform that breathes with the music — making the invisible texture of sound visible as a living background.
Emotion map
Every song has a mood. Audra translates it into color and shape — so you know how a song feels before a single note reaches you.
Animated lyrics
Words don't just appear — they move with the weight, pitch, and emotion of how they're sung. A whisper fades in softly. A belted note bursts large.
Rhythm grid
The skeleton of a song — bass, mid, high, treble — mapped to a visual pulse grid. See the structure of the beat, not just hear it.
Hearing profile
Tune the frequency boost to match your hearing aid's profile. Audra amplifies visually what your device filters out — a different channel for the same music.
